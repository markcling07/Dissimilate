Kotkot & Bukaw A Soft Climb

Two tiny mountains, one muddy trail, and the sweetest friends — turned into my happiest, most flowery little day.

Written with love by Allison

Into the Green

The trail was pretty from the very first step — leaves everywhere, soft light coming through the branches, and the kind of quiet that makes your shoulders relax. I kept stopping to look at everything. My friends teased me for being slow, but honestly the slow parts were my favorite.

The Cutest Trail Friends

Okay — the best surprise of the whole day. There were animals! A sweet little dog walked with us like he was our guide, and the cows just lounged in the grass like they owned the mountain. And the flowers? Tiny, everywhere, like the trail dressed up just for us.

"It wasn't about reaching the top fast — it was about who I was walking with."

A mandatory log pose.

Whenever they post their hike here, they always go to this log and take pictures.

Now, here I am.

If you happen to go on a hike to Mt. Kotkot & Mt. Bukaw, I declare that posing on this tree log is mandatory. Failure to do so may lead to unlimitted Kaltok.

Mt. Kotkot achieved
Now lemme take a rest,

Near the middle we stopped to breathe. I sat down, drank my water, and just looked out at all of it. That's the moment it hit me — I was actually doing this. Tired little me, halfway up two mountains, glowing and proud in the quietest, happiest way.

Climbing Bukaw

Then came the real climbing — up and up, one careful step at a time. My legs were shaking and my heart was pounding, but every time I looked back the view got a little prettier. I kept whispering to myself, you've got this, you've got this.

Coming down, my legs were noodles and my heart was full. I climbed up thinking I wasn't a mountain girl — and came down knowing maybe I am. Just a soft, slow, flower-picking, dog-petting kind. And honestly? That's my favorite kind to be.

— Allison
