The Ridge & The Ascent
Worth Every Step

This twin hike circuit was absolutely worth it. The mountains were incredibly picturesque, offering endless vantage points and perfect frames at every turn of the trail. The feature photo captures the stunning perspective of the rolling green ridges stretching into the afternoon mist, showing the long, winding path we were set to conquer.

My favorite part was definitely the descent — it was so fun to trail run, and it suddenly made me miss hiking Kimat. We got caught in the rain on the way down, but honestly, it didn’t lessen the experience at all. If anything, it made the whole adventure even more memorable.

Reaching the highest point of Mt. Kotkot at 1620 MASL reveals a weather-beaten wooden signpost. Standing alongside this marker and celebrating with friends marks the completion of the first summit. The surrounding views show vast green valleys and mist sliding across the mountains, with the afternoon sunbeams cutting through the clouds to illustrate the beautiful, undulating ridges that define this full circuit traverse.

The Falls Below Bukaw

Just below the Bukaw slopes sits a gorgeous, cold waterfall. A perfect spot to rest, wash away the trail mud, and capture the raw beauty of the water.

Primal Flow — The waterfall cascades down dark volcanic rocks, creating a cold, rushing pool at the bottom.

Hidden Sanctuary — Surrounded by thick rainforest foliage and wild shrubs, it forms a serene oasis.

Descending the slopes of Mt. Bukaw brings us to these hidden pools. The fast flow showcases the power of the mountain springs as they cascade down the cliffs. The long-exposure shot highlights the tranquil, clear waters of the pool itself, framed by deep green tropical leaves—a refreshing reward before facing the sudden trail run in the rain.
