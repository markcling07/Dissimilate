Above the clouds

Mount Pulag, one summit at a time

Luzon · 2,926 MASL · March 30

↓

Before the sun

we climbed in the dark

Mount Pulag is the highest mountain on the island of Luzon — 2,926 meters above the sea, the third-highest peak in the Philippines. Its summit is the one quiet place where the borders of Benguet, Ifugao, and Nueva Vizcaya all meet.

We set out at two in the morning on the 30th of March, headlamps on, the cold biting through every layer. People come here for one thing: the sea of clouds at sunrise — the view the locals call the playground of the gods. This is how the night went, hour by hour.

Follow the climb ↓

Two in the morning

We started in the dark

camp 1 · under the milky way

Headlamps on, layers zipped against the cold — we set out under a sky louder with stars than any of us had ever seen.

By five we were moving again, chasing the last dark up the grassland. Then the sky turned — grey, to rose, to a gold that spilled over everything.

Six in the morning

The summit

the playground of the gods

We reached the top just as the sun cleared the horizon, and the whole world below had vanished under a rolling white ocean. Two thousand nine hundred and twenty-six meters, the highest point on Luzon — and for a few minutes it felt like the only ground left in the sky.

The way down

The mossy forest

back by ten

In daylight the mountain became a different place. The trail dropped back into the mossy forest — every branch wrapped in green, the light falling soft through a canopy that has been growing undisturbed for longer than any of us have been alive.

“It is not the mountain we conquer, but ourselves.”

— Edmund Hillary
