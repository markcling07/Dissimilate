Mt. Apo

The Highest Peak in the Philippines 🏔️

via Kapatagan–Digos Trail
2,954 MASL

  • Major Climb
  • Difficulty 7/9
  • Trail Class 2/4
Kapatagan–Digos Trail · Davao del Sur

Every Filipino hiker has this one sitting at the top of their list. The Grandfather himself — Mt. Apo, 2,954 meters of mossy trails, boulders, fog, and cold via the Kapatagan–Digos trail. Difficulty 7/9, they said. They were not exaggerating. But after all the mountains that came before, it was finally time to meet the highest of them all.

01

Gudi-Gudi

First stop: Gudi-Gudi campsite. Yes, that is the real name — and no, I did not make it up. The welcome arch is right there for the doubters.

This is where the trail still lets you breathe before Apo starts asking the real questions. So we took our photos while our legs were still on speaking terms with us.

02

Camp beneath the crags

Camping right under the jagged crags of Apo hits different. Beautiful? Absolutely. But my brain at 2AM: what if there's an earthquake? What if a boulder decides today is the day? Then the fog rolls over the crags and suddenly everything is calm again. The mountain giveth anxiety, the mountain taketh it away.

03

The Boulder Field

So this is what I've been seeing online. Too steep, too sharp, too edgy, too dangerous. If you're a type of person that has strong intrusive, I wouldn't recommend going here.

04

The Night Push

Alarm before the sun, headlamps on, frost on the ferns — yes, frost, in the Philippines. We pushed for the summit in the dark and in the cold, some of us in shorts (you know who you are). Every step was a negotiation with my legs, right up until the sky started turning gold below us.

05

2,954

Then there it was. The sign. 2,954 meters above sea level — the literal top of the Philippines, and my head resting on it like a pillow I fully earned.

Every "why am I doing this" from the boulders and the night push just… vanished. It's like, finally. Finally, Apo.

06

The Lake

Past the summit sits the crater lake, quiet and still while the rain drew little circles on it. No fish, by the way — I checked so you don't have to. I wanted to walk the whole crater rim so bad, but the mountain said next time. Fine. Next time then.

07

The Company

This climb wouldn't have been the same without you. Here's to many more mountains for us to conquer together.
