Chapter One

The Ascent

The trail out of Bansalan begins under a roof of moss so thick the light turns green. July never lets you forget the rain — the path turns to raw clay, and we climb single file, one umbrella between us, a stray trail dog trotting behind. Nobody yet knows how much the mountain will ask. Here's to finding out — one boot at a time!

July 2, 2018

Here, time slows down — the highlands hold their own quiet lakes, dark tarns cupped in stone, the ridges rolling away in waves toward the far blue cone of Matutum.

APO

Chapter Three

The Summit

Near the top the trail becomes a chaos of grey boulders and the air turns sharp with sulfur — a reminder that Apo is a sleeping volcano, not merely a hill. And then the clouds themselves are below you, the world a rolling white sea. There's not much to say to that silence — just a small, sure sign that you made it.
