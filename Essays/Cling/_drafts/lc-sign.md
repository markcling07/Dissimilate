# LC Sign

**TL;DR:** LC Sign stumbled into one of the most effective marketing campaigns on the internet: Tony. His clips became memes, the memes became reach, and the reach — *I believe* — became profit. Now the company is trying to give other employees the same screentime by copying Tony's exact format, and it gets more awkward with every video. You can't photocopy a phenomenon.

*This is not to talk about LC Sign's business or their product, but their small tweak that all of us should be bothered by.*

*I assume you already know this guy, Tony, and what he does.*

## How did one employee become the whole brand?

By accident, and then by momentum. Tony's appearance on LC Sign's videos is so effective that *I believe* profits skyrocketed as clips of him reached parts of the world no signage company had any business reaching.

People now make compilations of Tony's clips — being reposted, reacted to, remixed everywhere. Tony has become a meme. Reacts, comments, and reposts are massive. Multiple accounts were created dedicated solely to Tony's videos, accounts that have absolutely nothing to do with LC Sign and everything to do with Tony. The company, of course, realized how successful this campaign was, so they keep making more videos — they have to ride the tide.

And here's the thing: LC Sign may not be a corporation so much as a big, growing business. They probably don't have a marketing team — it would be a waste of money. They're just creative. I'd even say the people doing the actual labour, the ones making the signs, get pulled out to help get Tony's video ready. That's the whole marketing department: Tony, a camera, and whoever's free.

## Why are the new videos with other people so awkward?

Because they're copies, and everyone can tell. I can imagine the conversation before they produced a video with another person talking aside from Tony:

1. They might have thought it would be more engaging if there were more people in the video; or
2. They are training someone to *supplement* Tony (not fire him — relax); or
3. I don't know.

Whatever the reason, this is where I feel awkward about the new clips. The new person just copies Tony — react to a funny clip, then pivot to the product. Same beats, same format, different face.

> The audience isn't looking at the new guy. They're looking *for* Tony.

Tony has done a lot of videos and is extremely effective in their marketing. He's gathered a fan base made up mostly of non-buyers, and that's fine — they knew the meme approach would work. But a meme is attached to a person, not a template. You can't onboard someone into being a phenomenon.

## So how do you give someone else screentime without the awkwardness?

Make the new face a standalone, not a Tony cover act. It would be far less awkward if the new character had a different style or approach — their own thing *that, of course,* works, *hopefully.* Different energy, different format, maybe a different corner of the business entirely.

Imagine the conversation before every new video now: *who's going to appear this time?* And every time it's another person doing exactly what Tony did in the last clip, the answer gets more uncomfortable.

The lesson for every business watching LC Sign: when lightning strikes one of your employees, don't hand the other employees a lightning costume. Build them their own weather.
