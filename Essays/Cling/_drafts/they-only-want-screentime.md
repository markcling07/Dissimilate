# They only want Screentime

**TL;DR:** When someone says something bold and controversial on camera, don't assume conviction. Most of the time it's a tactic — say the wildest thing in the room, get noticed, get invited back. Siding with plain logic doesn't get you a second appearance. The incentive isn't to be right; it's to be replayable.

## Why would anyone defend the indefensible on camera?

Because defending the indefensible is the fastest way to get noticed. Imagine defending a position nobody sane would touch — not because you believe it, but because you know every camera in the room just turned to you.

Get this: they are not NPA. They are not radicals, not true believers, not anything. This is just an opportunity to say something they thought would help them get approval — from a host, from a base, from an algorithm.

It's an all-in tactic. Say something bold, something controversial, and hopefully get invited next time to say more, or to defend the point you planted today. The outrage isn't the cost of the opinion. The outrage *is* the product.

## What happens if you just side with logic?

Nothing. That's the problem. Personally, if you side with the people, with plain logic, no one will notice you — because logic is what everyone already expects. It doesn't clip well. Nobody screenshots the reasonable guy. Nobody makes a compilation of measured takes.

> Being right is quiet. Being loud is a career.

The person calmly explaining the obvious gets ten seconds. The person saying something unhinged gets the headline, the reaction videos, the invite to come back and "clarify." Clarifying, of course, on another show, with more screentime.

## How do you spot a screentime play?

Look for the gap between the claim and the person. A screentime play usually has these tells:

- The take is dramatically bolder than anything they've said before.
- They benefit from the attention more than from the position — a show, a page, a product, a candidacy.
- When pressed, they don't defend the *idea*; they defend their right to say it.
- The walk-back comes later, quietly, somewhere with a smaller audience.

Real conviction is consistent and boring. Screentime plays are loud and disposable.

## So what do we do about it?

Stop rewarding it with the reaction. Every quote-post, every angry share, every "can you believe this guy" is a payment. They said something outrageous *so that* you would carry it further than they ever could alone.

The people siding with logic are still there. They're just not trending. Find them, because the loud ones were never talking to you anyway — they were talking to the camera.
